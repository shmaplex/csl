# Community Use Agreements (CUA) – Common Sense License (CSL) v1.0

This document provides **formal Community Use Agreement (CUA) templates** for organizations that wish to adopt the Common Sense License (CSL) v1.0.

These templates allow organizations to legally use, modify, and distribute CSL software while ensuring:

- Ethical compliance
- Contributions to the commons
- Protection of user rights
- Fairness and transparency across all user types

The templates cover the following cases:

1. Nonprofit / Educational Institutions
2. Small Business / Cooperative
3. Large Corporate / Revenue-Based
4. Other Custom Use Cases

---

## Instructions for Use

1. **Identify Your Category**  
   Determine which type best fits your organization: nonprofit, small business/cooperative, or large corporate.

2. **Fill In Template Fields**  
   Insert your organization’s name, software name, primary contacts, and contribution details (if applicable).

3. **Sign the Agreement**  
   Digitally or physically. Signatures from an authorized representative are required.

4. **Submit the Agreement**  
   Send completed agreements to the maintainers at `[maintainer@example.org]` or through the project website portal.

5. **Keep Records**  
   Retain a copy for internal compliance.

**Note:** If your organization does not fit neatly into these categories, see the “Custom Use Case” section below.

## Template 1: Nonprofit / Educational Institution

**Eligibility:**  
Nonprofits, schools, universities, and other educational or community-focused organizations.

**Agreement Template:**

```
Community Use Agreement – Nonprofit / Educational

This Agreement is entered into between [Institution Name] (“User”)
and the maintainers of [Software Name] (“Maintainers”).

1. License Grant:
   User may use, modify, and distribute the Software for nonprofit,
   educational, or community-oriented purposes under CSL v1.0.
2. Contributions:
   Optional: User is encouraged to contribute improvements,
   documentation, or modules back to the Commons Fund.
3. Reporting:
   User agrees to submit an annual acknowledgment of Software use
   to [Maintainer Contact].
4. Ethical Compliance:
   User affirms that Software will not be used for exploitative,
   surveillance, military, or environmentally harmful purposes.
5. Attribution:
   User will retain CSL license text and attribution in all copies.

Authorized Representative:
Name: _______________________
Title: _______________________
Signature: ___________________
Date: _______________________
```

## Template 2: Small Business / Cooperative

**Eligibility:**  
For-profit organizations under 50 employees or USD 5M annual revenue, cooperatives, and social enterprises.

**Agreement Template:**

```
Community Use Agreement – Small Business / Cooperative

This Agreement is entered into between [Organization Name] (“User”)
and the maintainers of [Software Name] (“Maintainers”).

1. License Grant:
   User may use, modify, and distribute the Software under CSL v1.0.
2. Contributions:
   Optional: User is encouraged to contribute improvements,
   documentation, or labor resources back to the commons.
3. Reporting:
   Voluntary annual report summarizing Software use and contributions
   to the Commons Fund.
4. Ethical Compliance:
   User affirms all use complies with CSL ethical-use restrictions.
5. Derivative Works:
   Any derivative work must be published under CSL v1.0.
6. Attribution:
   User will retain CSL license text and attribution in all copies.

Authorized Representative:
Name: _______________________
Title: _______________________
Signature: ___________________
Date: _______________________
```

## Template 3: Large Corporate / Revenue-Based

**Eligibility:**  
For-profit organizations exceeding 50 employees or USD 5M annual revenue.

**Agreement Template:**

```
Community Use Agreement – Large Corporate

This Agreement is entered into between [Organization Name] (“User”)
and the maintainers of [Software Name] (“Maintainers”).

1. License Grant:
   User may use, modify, and distribute the Software under CSL v1.0
   only after executing this Agreement.
2. Contribution Requirement:

- Minimum 5% of net Revenue derived from Software OR equivalent
  labor contribution (employee time improving the Software).
- Labor credit calculation methods to be agreed in writing with
  Maintainers.

3. Reporting:

- Annual, auditable report of Revenue or labor contributions.
- Reports submitted to Maintainers or independent auditor.

4. Derivative Works:
   All modifications, improvements, or derivative works must be released
   under CSL v1.0.
5. Ethical Compliance:
   Affirm that Software will not be used in violation of CSL ethical-use
   restrictions.
6. Audit & Verification:
   Maintainers may request documentation to verify contributions.
7. Termination:
   Non-compliance terminates all rights until fully remedied.
8. Attribution:
   Retain CSL license text and original author attribution in all copies.

Authorized Representative:
Name: _______________________
Title: _______________________
Signature: ___________________
Date: _______________________
```

## Template 4: Custom Use Case / Other Organizations

For organizations that do not fit the above categories (e.g., government agencies, foundations, international organizations):

**Instructions:**

1. Describe your organization and intended use.
2. Provide contact information and point of responsibility.
3. Propose contribution structure (monetary, labor, or resource-based).
4. Submit for review to `[Maintainer Contact]`.
5. Maintainers will respond with a **custom Community Use Agreement**, ensuring ethical compliance and contribution alignment.

**Agreement Template (Custom):**

Community Use Agreement – Custom

[Customized agreement drafted in collaboration with Maintainers,
following CSL principles: ethical use, contribution to the Commons Fund,
attribution, and reporting.]

---

## Additional Notes

- All CUAs are **binding upon execution**.
- Organizations are encouraged to **retain a copy** of all submitted agreements.
- Templates are designed to **protect both Users and the Commons**, ensure fairness, and provide clear legal recourse in case of disputes.
- Use of CSL without a signed CUA (if required) by a Large User is considered **non-compliant**.
- Maintainers may **audit compliance** if needed to protect the commons.
